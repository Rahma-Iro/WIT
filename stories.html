Real Stories

Learn from real cybersecurity incidents

The Fake Scholarship Link

What happened: Maria received an email about a scholarship opportunity. She clicked the link and entered her personal information.

The problem: The email was a phishing scam. Hackers used her information to open credit cards in her name.

The lesson: Always verify emails by checking the sender's address and visiting websites directly instead of clicking links.

The Hacked Instagram Account

What happened: Ahmed used the same password for Instagram, Facebook, and his email. When Instagram got hacked, he lost access to everything.

The problem: Using the same password everywhere means one hack compromises all accounts.

The lesson: Use unique, strong passwords for each account and consider using a password manager.

The Free Wi-Fi Trap

What happened: Zainab connected to "CoffeeFree_WiFi" at a café and checked her bank account. Her login credentials were stolen.

The problem: The Wi-Fi network was fake, set up by hackers to intercept user data.

The lesson: Never do banking or enter passwords on public Wi-Fi. Use a VPN or wait for a secure connection.

The Social Media Overshare

What happened: Chioma posted her location, hometown, pet's name, and birthday on social media. A hacker used this information to answer her security questions and hack her email.

The problem: Information shared online is public and can be used against you.

The lesson: Be careful about personal information you share online. Hackers use it to answer security questions.

The Malware Download

What happened: Kunle downloaded a free movie from an untrusted website. His computer was infected with malware that stole his files and passwords.

The problem: Downloading from untrusted sources is dangerous. The file contained hidden malicious code.

The lesson: Only download from official sources. Use antivirus software and keep it updated.
