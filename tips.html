Security Tips

15 practical tips to keep you safe online

Password Safety

Use Strong Passwords

Create passwords with uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols. Make them at least 12 characters long.

Never Reuse Passwords

Use different passwords for each account. If one account gets hacked, others stay safe.

Use a Password Manager

Tools like Bitwarden or 1Password help you store passwords securely without memorizing them.

Phishing & Scams

Check Email Senders

Verify the email address carefully. Hackers often use similar-looking addresses to trick you.

Don't Click Suspicious Links

Hover over links to see the actual URL before clicking. Don't click links in unexpected emails.

Verify Requests for Information

Banks never ask for passwords via email. Call them directly if you're unsure.

Social Media Safety

Limit Personal Information

Don't share your address, phone number, or birthdate publicly on social media.

Check Privacy Settings

Make your account private and control who can see your posts.

Be Careful with Friend Requests

Verify the person before accepting. Fake accounts can be used for scams.

Public Wi-Fi Safety

Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Sensitive Tasks

Don't do banking or enter passwords on public Wi-Fi. Wait until you're on a secure network.

Use a VPN

Virtual Private Networks encrypt your connection, keeping your data safe on public networks.

Turn Off Auto-Connect

Disable automatic Wi-Fi connection to prevent connecting to fake networks.

Device Protection

Keep Software Updated

Updates fix security vulnerabilities. Enable automatic updates when possible.

Use Antivirus Software

Install reputable antivirus software and keep it up to date.

Enable Two-Factor Authentication

Add an extra security layer by requiring a second form of verification when logging in.
